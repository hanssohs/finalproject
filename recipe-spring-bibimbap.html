Korean Bibimbap

üå∏ Spring üç≥ Weekend Medium
PREP TIME30 mins
COOK TIME20 mins
TOTAL TIME50 mins
SERVINGS2
BUDGET$6

Description

Bibimbap means 'mixed rice' in Korean. This colorful bowl features seasoned vegetables, protein, a fried egg, and spicy gochujang sauce. Fun to make a

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice (short grain is best)
  • 2 eggs
  • 8 oz protein (ground beef, tofu, or chicken)
  • 1 cup spinach
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 zucchini, julienned
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Sesame oil
  • Soy sauce
  • FOR GOCHUJANG SAUCE:
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions

  1. Make gochujang sauce: Mix all sauce ingredients until smooth. Set aside.
  2. Season protein: If using ground beef, cook with garlic, soy sauce, and sesame oil until browned. Set aside.
  3. Prepare vegetables (season each separately for authentic flavor):
  4. Spinach: Blanch in boiling water for 1 minute, squeeze dry, season with sesame oil and salt.
  5. Bean sprouts: Blanch for 2 minutes, drain, season with sesame oil and salt.
  6. Carrot: Saut√© in a bit of oil until softened, season with salt.
  7. Zucchini: Saut√© until softened, season with salt and garlic.
  8. Fry eggs sunny-side up with runny yolks.
  9. Assemble: Place rice in bowls. Arrange vegetables and protein in sections on top. Add fried egg in center.
  10. Serve with gochujang sauce. Mix everything together before eating!

üí° Student Tips

Authentic Touch: Serve in a hot stone bowl (dolsot) if you have one - creates crispy rice at the bottom!

Meal Prep Friendly: Prep all vegetables on Sunday, assemble bowls throughout the week.

Gochujang Substitute: Can't find gochujang? Mix sriracha with a little miso paste and sugar.
