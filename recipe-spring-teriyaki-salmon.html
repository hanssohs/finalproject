Teriyaki Salmon Bowl

üå∏ Spring üç≥ Weekend Medium
PREP TIME20 mins
COOK TIME25 mins
TOTAL TIME45 mins
SERVINGS2
BUDGET$8

Description

Restaurant-quality teriyaki salmon is easier than you think! The homemade teriyaki sauce is sweet, savory, and glossy. Serve over rice with edamame fo

Ingredients

  • 2 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 1 cup shelled edamame
  • 1 cup sliced cucumber
  • FOR TERIYAKI SAUCE:
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin (or 2 tbsp sugar + 2 tbsp water)
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch mixed with 1 tbsp water
  • Sesame seeds and green onions for garnish

Instructions

  1. Make teriyaki sauce: Combine soy sauce, mirin, brown sugar, vinegar, garlic, and ginger in a small pot. Bring to a simmer.
  2. Add cornstarch slurry and stir until sauce thickens (about 2 minutes). Set aside half for serving.
  3. Pat salmon dry with paper towels. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Heat oil in a non-stick pan over medium-high. Place salmon skin-side up and cook for 4 minutes.
  5. Flip salmon and brush with teriyaki sauce. Cook another 4-5 minutes until cooked through.
  6. Continue brushing with sauce for a beautiful glaze.
  7. Meanwhile, cook edamame in boiling salted water for 5 minutes. Drain.
  8. Assemble bowls: rice, salmon, edamame, cucumber. Drizzle with remaining sauce.
  9. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onions!

üí° Student Tips

Don't Overcook: Salmon should be slightly pink in the center - it continues cooking after you remove it from heat.

Budget Option: Can't find mirin? Use 2 tbsp sugar + 2 tbsp water as substitute.

Make it Stretch: One batch of sauce can glaze 4 salmon fillets - make extra for meal prep!
